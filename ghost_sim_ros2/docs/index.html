Hardware-free preparation is complete

The remaining work is execution and evidence—not inventing protocols after seeing results. Every major collection, analysis, integrity and release stage now has tooling and tests.

01 · HARDWARE

USB BOM & inventory

Machine-readable BOM, public page, interfaces, ten-photo plan and privacy-separated exact device capture.

02 · COLLECTION

Controlled covariance

One-terminal 90-second run, V4L2 readbacks, fixed 15–75 s analysis, subwindows and operator attestation.

03 · TRUTH

Grid validation

Six-point bias/RMSE analysis, error vectors, measured-vs-truth view and discrete spatial error map.

04 · OPERATIONS

Local trial conductor

Balanced 55-slot randomization, visual/audio cues, high-resolution timing and server-side event logs.

05 · AUDIT

Immutable campaign state

Pinned plan and order remain unchanged; outcomes and amendments live in separate audited records.

06 · ANALYSIS

Paired statistics & plots

Gap filtering, endpoint/reacquisition metrics, confidence intervals, failures and median-like representative runs.

07 · PERFORMANCE

USB timing & Pi resources

Frame interarrival jitter, latency diagnostics, CPU, RSS, memory, temperature and load characterization.

08 · INTEGRITY

Evidence archives

Non-overwriting ZIP packages, required-artifact profiles, SHA-256 manifests and tamper verification.

09 · LOCK

Parameter identity

Git revision, estimator/protocol files, calibration, camera controls and runtime parameters are hash-locked.

10 · RELEASE

Claims gate

Machine-readable validated, behavior-only, software-only, pending and prohibited public statements.

11 · SESSION

Dependency-gated runbook

Eleven phases prevent grid, campaign, release or teardown before prerequisite evidence exists.

12 · SHOWCASE

Three-take hero protocol

All takes retained, objective eligibility checks and clarity-based selection rather than lowest numerical error.

Next physical sequence

The camera session follows one controlled chain. A failed gate preserves evidence and blocks downstream claims.

1
Inventory the actual USB setupCapture exact Pi/webcam identity, camera modes, controls, calibration hash and privacy-reviewed photographs.
2
Lock geometry and collect controlled RRigid camera/tag, unchanged lighting, 90-second collection, fixed-window quality gate.
3
Execute the six-point physical gridSame camera setup; publish setup-specific bias, RMSE, repeatability and discrete spatial errors.
4
Dry-run all six campaign conditionsVerify cue timing, raw logs, endpoints, measured gaps, state updates and analysis before formal evidence.
5
Lock parameters and initialize 55 slotsHash all relevant files/configuration and freeze the balanced randomized order before outcomes are observed.
6
Collect paired trials and runtime evidenceAccept/reject audibly, preserve failures, characterize Pi load and USB timing on representative blocks.
7
Record three hero takesPreserve all takes and select for explanatory clarity under the predeclared eligibility rule.
8
Analyze, package, verify and releaseGenerate condition-specific evidence, verify checksums, then promote only claims that pass the release matrix.
Current public boundary: GHOST does not yet claim validated real-world tracking accuracy, statistically proven IMM superiority, general object tracking, production robustness, PX4/HIL integration, real vehicle command, flight readiness or flight testing.
